The five non-negotiables

loose by default

Tension is the speed cap. Tense only a stabilizer (curl the pinky), never the tapping fingers.

tap the surface

Don't bottom out. Let each tap rebound into the next — light, shallow contact cycles faster than full presses.

rotate, don't hammer

Alternation is a forearm doorknob-rock. The rotation alternates; the fingers just ride it.

+5 only when clean

Advance by five only when it's even AND loose for 25+ notes. Never skip a step.

stop at pain

Joint pain = tension warning, reset don't push. Pain that lingers off the keyboard, or any tingling / numbness — rest it and see a doctor. No rank is worth a wrecked hand.

01

Warm-up

5–10 min · every session

Never start cold. This is most of the fix for the "my stamina vanished after a break" feeling — it didn't vanish, it's cold and rusty. Tick through it, then play.

02

The rotation drill

the core new skill

This builds alternation as its own skill — you can't bolt it onto singletap muscle memory, which is why forcing it tends to fall apart and strain the fingers. Build the clean version from slow.

  1. Rest index + middle on the two keys, relaxed arch. Not the same as your singletap grip — this is a new, dedicated hand position.
  2. Find the doorknob rock: slowly rotate the forearm so the press tips index → middle → index. The rotation does the alternating; the fingers ride it.
  3. Start at 120. The goal is not speed — both taps must feel identical and loose.
  4. Hold 25+ notes clean, then +5. Clean and loose is the only ticket up.
  5. Fingers lock or a joint complains → stop, shake the hand out, restart slower. That's tension creeping back.
  6. Beat your best clean BPM? Log it in the tracker below.
03

Raising the ceiling

end of session

Burst trainerspeed

Push the top end without mashing your form to bits.

  1. Pick burst-heavy maps ~20–30 BPM above comfort.
  2. Each burst: max constant tapping, stream just over your limit. No random mashing, no mid-burst adjusting.
  3. Do it until tired — best at the end of a session.
  4. ~25 clean notes at a BPM = you own it. A 10-note peak doesn't count.

Dotted rhythmevenness

Pure piano drill for spacing + fast transitions.

  1. Tap the stream long–short–long–short.
  2. Then reverse: short–long–short–long.
  3. It forces even spacing and trains the quick transition. 2–3 min.
  4. Bring it back to straight even taps — it'll feel smoother.

04

8-week progression

4–6 days/wk · ~25–40 min

Targets are clean alternation — 25+ notes, 95%+ acc, loose. Tap a chip to load that tempo. Speed is the slowest skill to raise; plateaus are normal, not failure.
